Rules

Five balls (atoms) are secretly placed on a checkerboard of 8 × 8 boxes. We do not see these balls on the board, the goal of the player is to find out where these balls are in as few shots as possible.

A shot consists in sending an imaginary X-ray from one of the 32 entry squares and to see where he will come out. If he touches a ball, he does not leave the checkerboard. If it touches a ball by one of the corners, it will be deflected at right angles. If it does not touch or brush anything, it simply stands out from the other side.

The player then places pawns on grids at the supposed places of the atoms and validates his choice.


Thus, X-rays can behave essentially in four ways:

  • They can cross the game without being modified in their straight path if they do not pass close to an atom.
  • They can be deflected 90º if they pass near an atom
  • They can be absorbed and not emerge if they encounter an atom directly (also if they reach it after being diverted previously).
  • And they can be reflected just before entering a square adjacent to a busy one.
  • The red ray is deviated by the atom a.
  • The violet ray is absorbed by the atom a.
  • The blue ray is absorbed by the atom before it is deflected by the atom b. Absorption dominates.
  • The brown line is deflected by the atom at (90 °) and by the atom b (other 90 °) and so it appears reflected by the same square through which it entered.
  • The green ray is reflected by the atom c at the time of entry.

The goal of the game is to guess the position of atoms with a minimum of shooting.
